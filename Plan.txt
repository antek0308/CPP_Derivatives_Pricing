
I decided to build my repo form the start with princpiles learned in the classes.
I know that it would have taken more times, but I actually wanted to learn to extrapolate the ideas
and uild custom solution on my own. As I had a little bit more time, I decided to go
this path.

I wanted to build this project QuantLib style as I think that this i gold standard in terms
of instrument pricing in banks and everywehre else.

What this entailed is that the some of classes were retired, some of them resusd, but in genral
low level code was kept.


Payoff            (abstract)  evaluate() + strike()/type()      ← rich enough for ALL engines
   └─ PlainVanillaPayoff
Instrument        (abstract)  holds a Payoff; setPricingEngine(); NPV()
   ├─ EuropeanOption
   └─ BarrierOption           adds barrier level + up/down + in/out
StochasticProcess (abstract)  the dynamics + market
   └─ BlackScholesProcess     spot, r, d, vol     [HestonProcess later]
PricingEngine     (abstract)  calculate() → a price             ← the "strategy"
   ├─ AnalyticEuropeanEngine  reuses your bs_price
   ├─ MonteCarloEngine        simulates the process
   └─ FiniteDifferenceEngine  (future, PDE)
